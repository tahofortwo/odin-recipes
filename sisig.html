Odin Recipes

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish from the Pampanga region, typically made from chopped parts of a pig's head (like the jowl and ears) and chicken liver.

Ingredients

  • Pork Cheel
  • Pork Ear
  • Chicken Liver
  • Onions
  • Chilis
  • Salt
  • Calamansi
  • Crushed Peppercorns
  • Soy Sauce
  • Ground Pepper
  • Butter

How to Cook Sisig

  1. Boil the Meat: In a pot, cover the pork belly and pig ears with water. Add salt, whole peppercorns, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then simmer for about 1 hour until the meat is tender but not mushy.

  2. Crisp the Pork: Drain and let the meat air-dry. Deep fry the pork in hot oil until golden brown and crispy (like lechon kawali), or broil in an oven until the skin crackles.

  3. Chop: Let the meat cool down, then chop it into small, uniform cubes.

  4. Prepare the Liver:

  5. Combine: In a large mixing bowl, toss together the chopped pork, mashed liver, onions, chili peppers, soy sauce, calamansi juice, and mayonnaise.

  6. Serve Sizzling: Melt a tablespoon of butter or margarine on a cast-iron sizzling plate over medium heat. Add the sisig mixture and let it cook for about 1-2 minutes until it sizzles. Crack an egg on top, mix, and serve immediately with hot rice!
