Adobo

Philippine adobo is the unofficial national dish of the Philippines and a beloved cooking method. It involves marinating and simmering meat, seafood, or vegetables in a savory, tangy blend of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns

Ingredients

  • Soy Sauce
  • Cane Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Bay Leaves
  • Peppercorns

How to Cook Adobo

  1. Marinate the MeatCombine the chicken or pork, half of the minced garlic, soy sauce, and black peppercorns in a large bowl. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to overnight in the fridge).

  2. Sear the MeatHeat cooking oil in a heavy pot or deep pan over medium-high heat. Remove the meat from the marinade (save the marinade for later) and sear the meat in the pan for 1–2 minutes per side until lightly browned

  3. Add the remaining minced garlic to the pot and sauté for about a minute. Pour in the reserved marinade, water, and bay leaves. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer.

  4. Simmer until the meat is fully cooked and tender. This takes about 25 to 30 minutes for chicken and about 45 to 60 minutes for pork

  5. Remove the lid for the last 10 minutes of cooking to allow the sauce to reduce into a rich, flavorful glaze. Taste the sauce and adjust with salt if needed. Serve hot over a generous bowl of steamed white rice
