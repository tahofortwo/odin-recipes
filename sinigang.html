Sinigang

Sinigang is a popular Filipino soup or stew defined by its signature sour and savory taste

Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Radish
  • Tamarind Mix
  • Green Chili
  • Fish Sauce
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes
  • String Beans
  • Water
  • Okra
  • Kangkong Leaves

How to Cook Sinigang

  1. Sear the Pork: In a large, deep pot, heat a little oil and brown the pork. Add the onions and half of the tomatoes, sautéing until soft

  2. Boil and Simmer: Add the water, fish sauce, and peppercorns. Bring to a boil and skim off any gray scum that floats to the top. Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for 45 to 60 minutes until the pork is tender.

  3. Add Souring Mix: Stir in the tamarind (sinigang) mix. (If using fresh tamarind, strain the juice into the pot now). Adjust the sourness to your liking.

  4. Add Vegetables: Drop in the radish and simmer for 5 minutes. Then add the eggplant, okra, and string beans, along with the long green peppers. Cover and cook for 5 to 7 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender

  5. Add Kangkong: Stir in the remaining tomatoes and the kangkong leaves. Submerge the leaves in the broth, turn off the heat, cover, and let it steam for 3 minutes.

  6. Serve: Taste the broth, add more fish sauce if needed, and serve hot with steaming white rice.
